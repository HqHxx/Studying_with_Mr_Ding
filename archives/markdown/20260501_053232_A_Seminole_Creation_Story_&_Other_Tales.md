### 双语正文

This **article** shares **authentic** Seminole traditional tales, whose **title** honors the **original** oral stories passed down for centuries. The Seminole **nation**, a proud Indigenous **minority** and longtime **resident** of the U.S. Southeast, found **refuge** in Florida’s fertile **soil** in the 1700s while fleeing **turbulent** wars to the north. They were later joined by escaped enslaved people who became their close **ally** as the Black Seminole community. The Seminole refused to **concede** their lands to the U.S. government, never to **abandon** their homelands even during the brutal Seminole Wars of the 19th century, earning the self-given name Yat'siminoli, a **translation** meaning “Unconquered People” that embodies their unbroken **dignity** and **perpetual** respect for the natural world. Their **faith** in the Creator’s plan guides every part of daily life, and their spiritual **conception** of the world **reflects** deep ties to **ecology**, with stories that **weave** together human, animal, and divine realms to **transmit** core values across generations.

本文分享了**真实可信的**塞米诺尔族传统故事，故事的**标题**是为了致敬数百年来口耳相传的**原始**口述版本。塞米诺尔**民族**是充满自豪感的原住民**少数群体**，也是美国东南部的长期**居民**，18世纪时他们为躲避北方的**动荡**战乱，在佛罗里达的肥沃**土地**上找到了**避难所**。后来逃脱奴役的黑人也加入了他们，成为塞米诺尔人的亲密**盟友**，共同组成了黑塞米诺尔社群。塞米诺尔人拒绝向美国政府**割让**土地，哪怕是在19世纪残酷的塞米诺尔战争期间也从未**抛弃**故土，因此给自己取名为Yat'siminoli，**翻译**过来意为“不可征服的民族”，这个名字体现了他们从未受损的**尊严**，以及对自然世界**永恒的**敬畏。他们对创世主安排的**信仰**指引着日常生活的方方面面，他们的精神世界观**反映出**与**生态学**的深刻联结，故事将人类、动物和神界**编织**在一起，代代**传承**核心价值观。

These tales **focus** on the **goodness** of the Creator, the collective duty to **protect** the land that sustains all life, and timeless lessons for the **future**. For the Seminole, Florida’s sprawling landscapes are no empty **garden** but a living entity that **yields** food, medicine, and shelter, so **foremost** among their values is reciprocal care for all living beings. The first of the three most well-known stories details the creation of the world: when the Grandfather of all things finished shaping the earth, he placed every animal inside a giant shell resting atop the highest mountains, just at the **threshold** of the world’s official completion. He **commended** the panther, Coo-wah-Chobee, for its quiet patience and unmatched strength, choosing it to be the first creature to walk the new earth. When a tree root eventually cracked the shell, the panther waited too long to move, so Wind, a **faithful** servant of the Creator, helped the panther exit first, followed by birds, bears, deer, snakes, otters, and thousands of other creatures. The Creator then established a **framework** of roles for each being: the panther clan would hold sacred knowledge of law and healing, Wind would serve all life as the air all creatures breathe, and birds would oversee that all beings occupy their right place on the land. The story emphasizes that **cooperative** care for one another and the natural world is the only path to long-term survival.

这些故事**聚焦**于创世主的**善**、**保护**孕育所有生命的土地的集体责任，以及面向**未来**的永恒教训。对塞米诺尔人来说，佛罗里达广袤的土地不是空荡的**花园**，而是能**出产**食物、药材和居所的活的生命体，因此他们价值观中**最重要的**一点就是对所有生命的互惠关怀。三个最知名的故事里，第一个详细讲述了世界的起源：当万物之祖完成了地球的塑造后，他把所有动物都放在一个巨壳里，巨壳安放在最高的山顶，正处于世界正式完工的**临界点**。他**称赞**黑豹库瓦乔比沉静耐心、力量无双，选它作为第一个行走在新地球上的生物。后来树根撑裂了巨壳，黑豹却迟迟没有挪动，因此创世主的**忠实**仆人“风”帮助黑豹第一个走了出来，跟在后面的是鸟类、熊、鹿、蛇、水獭和成千上万种其他生物。随后创世主为每个生命都设立了角色**框架**：黑豹部族将掌握律法和疗愈的神圣知识，风会作为所有生物呼吸的空气为众生服务，鸟类则负责监督所有生灵都在土地上占据自己应有的位置。这个故事强调，**协作**关照彼此和自然世界是长期生存的唯一路径。

The second tale explains the origin of the night sky, which **local** Seminole **view** as a spirit path for deceased community members. Good souls travel the Milky Way to the City of the West, while bad souls remain trapped under **bush** and earth, never to walk the mortal world again. The Seminole say the Milky Way glows brighter after a beloved community member passes, lighting the path for their journey. This story carries an **underlying** message: one must live with integrity, speak with a kind **voice**, never steal or lie, and act bravely for their people to earn a place on the spirit path. Even during solar eclipse events, the community comes together to make loud noise to scare off the toad-frog that bites the sun, a tradition that **registers** their collective will to **support** one another against unseen harms. They do not **retreat** from challenges, using their collective **ability** to face whatever threat comes their way.

第二个故事解释了夜空的起源，**当地的**塞米诺尔人**认为**夜空是离世族人的灵魂通道。善良的灵魂会沿着银河前往西方之城，而邪恶的灵魂会被困在**灌木**和泥土之下，永远无法再踏入人间。塞米诺尔人说，当深受爱戴的族人离世后，银河会变得更亮，为他们的旅途照亮道路。这个故事隐含着一个**深层**信息：人必须品行正直、言语**温和**友善，不偷盗不撒谎，勇敢为族人付出，才能在灵魂通道上赢得一席之地。哪怕是发生日食的时候，整个社群都会聚在一起制造巨响，吓跑啃食太阳的蟾蜍，这个传统**彰显**了他们互相**支持**、共同对抗未知伤害的集体意志。他们面对挑战从不**退缩**，会用集体的**能力**迎接任何到来的威胁。

The third tale follows Chok-fee, a skilled young hunter whose grandmother warns him not to travel south, but curiosity leads him to a hidden pair of lodges deep in the forest. The headless man inside greets him with **warm** **hospitality**, explaining that an Unga, a giant man-faced bird that is his sworn **enemy**, stole his head to force him to allow the Unga to marry his daughter. Chok-fee agrees to help, tracks the Unga to a tall cypress tree, kills it with his arrow, and returns the head just before midday to save the man’s life. As a reward, the man tells Chok-fee that all deer in the land are his, that he will never go hungry if he waits four full days before entering the small lodge where his daughter waits. But Chok-fee’s curiosity gets the better of him, and he peeks inside on the third day. The girl vanishes into the sky to become the Morning Star, and the gift of **saving** the effort of long hunting trips is lost forever. If Chok-fee had waited, the Seminole would have all they need just by wishing for it; instead, they work hard to hunt and tend the land, a lesson that no shortcut ever yields lasting rewards.

第三个故事的主角是年轻的优秀猎人乔克菲，他的祖母警告他不要往南走，但好奇心还是驱使他走到了森林深处两座隐秘的小屋前。屋里的无头男子**热情好客**地接待了他，解释说自己的死**敌**昂加（一种长着人脸的巨鸟）偷走了他的头，逼他同意把女儿嫁给昂加。乔克菲同意帮忙，一路追踪昂加到了一棵高大的柏树上，用箭射死了昂加，赶在正午前把头颅送了回去，救了男子的命。作为回报，男子告诉乔克菲，这片土地上的所有鹿都属于他，只要他等满四天再走进女儿所在的小屋，就永远不会挨饿，还能省去长途狩猎的力气。但乔克菲没能克制住好奇心，第三天就往屋里偷看了。女孩消失在天空中变成了启明星，这份能**省去**长途狩猎辛劳的礼物也就永远丢失了。如果乔克菲当初愿意等待，塞米诺尔人只要许愿就能得到所有需要的东西；但事与愿违，他们必须辛勤狩猎、耕种土地，这个故事告诉我们，没有任何捷径能带来长久的回报。

These **literary** treasures are a vital **source** of Seminole cultural heritage that **include** lessons that resonate far beyond their community. They remind readers that Indigenous stories are not just myth, but living records of how to live in harmony with the natural world. Today, cultural preservation groups and scholars alike **support** efforts to document and share these stories, as they offer important insights into sustainable living for people across the globe. The Seminole have never **yielded** their right to their land and culture, continuing to share their stories to keep their heritage alive for generations to come.

这些**文学**瑰宝是塞米诺尔文化遗产的重要**来源**，**包含**的道理能引发远超出其社群范围的共鸣。它们提醒读者，原住民故事不只是神话，更是如何与自然世界和谐共处的活的记录。如今，文化保护组织和学者都**支持**记录和分享这些故事的工作，因为它们能为全球人类的可持续生活提供重要的启发。塞米诺尔人从未**放弃**对自己土地和文化的权利，他们会继续分享这些故事，让自己的文化遗产世世代代传承下去。
